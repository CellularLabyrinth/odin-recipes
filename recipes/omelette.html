Omelette

Description

When I've rolled out of bed in the morning and can't bear to face the day, I tend to find some fatty food is a good first step to chase the morning grog away. I try to avoid carbohydrates when possible, so the omelette has become indispensible in my culinary reportoire.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Crack two eggs into a bowl, stir to mix
  2. Add a little bit of butter to a pan and place over heat until the butter has melted and is bubbling
  3. Pour the egg mixture over the butter
  4. Once the bottom of the egg mixture had cooked and is solid, use a spatula to lift one end of the omelette over the other, keeping the uncooked egg mixture inside like an envelope
  5. Immediately flip the omelette over and cook until the outside is sealed (I prefer not to cook the inside of the omelette too much!)
  6. You may want to brush some butter off the omelette with a paper towel
  7. Add a little salt, and a lot of pepper
